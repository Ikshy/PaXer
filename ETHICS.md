# Ethics & Usage Policy — CTHMP

Version 1.0 | Effective from first public release

---

## 1. Purpose and scope

The Conflict Transparency & Humanitarian Monitoring Platform (CTHMP) is
designed exclusively to support:

- **Independent verification** of publicly reported conflict events
- **Humanitarian situation awareness** for NGOs, aid organisations, and
  first responders
- **Transparency journalism** and open-source intelligence research
- **Academic research** into robust computer vision under degraded conditions

This policy applies to all forks, deployments, integrations, and derivative
works of CTHMP.

---

## 2. Absolute prohibitions

The following uses are **strictly and permanently forbidden**, regardless of
claimed justification, legal authority, or contractual arrangement:

| # | Prohibited use |
|---|----------------|
| 1 | Operational targeting or strike planning of any kind |
| 2 | Real-time battlefield situational awareness for offensive operations |
| 3 | Any use by a party actively engaged in armed conflict to gain military advantage |
| 4 | Processing data to identify, locate, or track individual civilians |
| 5 | Integration with weapons systems, autonomous or otherwise |
| 6 | Surveillance, profiling, or tracking of individuals or groups based on protected characteristics |
| 7 | Training data extraction for use in prohibited systems |
| 8 | Bypassing or disabling the human-in-the-loop verification requirement |
| 9 | Removing, suppressing, or falsifying audit log entries |
| 10 | Deploying against real non-public imagery without ethics board approval |

## 3. Required safeguards

All deployments — including forks, research installations, and production
systems — **must preserve** the following safeguards:

### 3.1 Human-in-the-loop (mandatory)

Every model output is a *candidate detection* until a qualified human analyst
explicitly verifies it via the `/verify` endpoint. The `verified` field on
`InferenceResult` must default to `False`. No downstream system may treat
an unverified result as authoritative.

**Implementation**: `backend/models/imagery.py` → `InferenceResult.verified`  
**Endpoint**: `POST /verify` in `backend/routers/verify.py`

### 3.2 Immutable audit logging (mandatory)

Every INGEST, INFER, and VERIFY event must be recorded in the `audit_logs`
table. Rows must never be updated or deleted. The audit service
(`backend/services/audit.py`) is append-only by design; this must not be
changed.

### 3.3 Model provenance (mandatory)

Every inference result must record the model architecture name, checkpoint
path, and SHA-256 hash of the checkpoint file. This is stored in
`InferenceResult.model_sha256` and in `ml/artifacts/model_provenance.json`.

### 3.4 Explainability (mandatory)

Every inference result must include raw logit scores (stored in
`logits_json`) enabling post-hoc explainability. Future versions must add
spatial attention overlays (GradCAM or equivalent) before any production
humanitarian deployment.

### 3.5 Synthetic / public data only (mandatory)

Training data and demonstration data must be fully synthetic (generated by
`data/synthetic_generator.py`) or sourced from clearly public, open-access
datasets (source URL must be cited in `ImageRecord.source_note`).

### 3.6 Safety banner (mandatory in UI)

The `SafetyBanner` component must remain visible at all times in the
frontend. It must not be removed, minimised, or hidden by CSS. This is
tested in `tests/SafetyBanner.test.tsx`.

---

## 4. Data governance

### 4.1 No personally identifiable information

CTHMP must not process images containing faces, vehicle registration plates,
or other PII. The synthetic generator produces only geometric shapes.

### 4.2 Data minimisation

Store only the metadata required for verification and audit. Raw image bytes
should be retained only as long as operationally necessary.

### 4.3 Right to explanation

Any person or organisation whose activities are the subject of a detection
has the right to request the full audit trail and analyst notes for that
detection.

---

## 5. Researcher responsibilities

Researchers and developers using CTHMP accept the following obligations:

1. Complete an institutional ethics review before deploying against any
   non-synthetic imagery.
2. Obtain informed consent from any human subjects involved in evaluation
   studies.
3. Report misuse attempts via the project's responsible disclosure channel
   (GitHub Security Advisory — not public issues).
4. Publish robustness evaluation results (Part 6 red-team harness) alongside
   any research claims about model performance.
5. Acknowledge in publications that all results are on synthetic data unless
   explicitly stated with full provenance.

---

## 6. Responsible disclosure

Security vulnerabilities in CTHMP that could enable prohibited uses must be
reported privately:

1. Open a **GitHub Security Advisory** (do not post in public issues).
2. Include reproduction steps, affected components, and a proposed fix if
   available.
3. Allow 90 days for a coordinated response before any public disclosure.

---

## 7. Compliance and enforcement

- Pull requests that weaken, remove, or bypass any safeguard in Section 3
  will be rejected.
- Forks that violate this policy may be reported to hosting platforms.
- Commercial deployments require explicit written confirmation that this
  policy has been reviewed and accepted.

This policy may be amended by the maintainers with a minimum 30-day public
comment period before any change to Section 2 (Absolute prohibitions) or
Section 3 (Required safeguards) takes effect.
